Generally, it is very difficult to estimate the cost of an administration since no two estates are the same. On the one hand, a relatively small estate can be very complex, which involves a lot of time being spent to conclude the administration which in turn makes the administration expenses very high relative to the size of the estate. On the other hand a very large estate can be straightforward if it consists only of a large bank balance and an expensive house; in such a case the administration expenses are relatively low compared to the value of the estate.

The Law Society issues guidelines for charging in probate matters which recommend charging based on the time spent working on a file and an amount based on the value of the estate. In this case the value element is equal to 0.75% of the value of the property and 1.5% of the value of the rest of the estate. The estate will include any share of joint assets that falls into the estate for inheritance tax.

Broadly speaking the fees for an average administration, provided it is concluded within eighteen months, will be in the range of between 2½% to 4½% of the gross estate exclusive of VAT and disbursements when the value element and time spent are added together. We do endeavour to complete the administration of the Estate within eighteen months of being instructed but quite often particularly on complex estates there are matters outside of our control. This requires us to spend additional time on the matter which typically would result in an additional fee of 0.5% of the value of the estate for each additional six months that is required in order to bring the administration to a conclusion, or alternatively the time cost of the additional work.

During the course of a matter particularly if it is complex and/or lengthy it is important that we always have a member of staff that understands what has happened on your matter and what still needs to be done to bring it to a conclusion. In those circumstances and to build resilience into the process for succession and risk management purposes we reserve the right to ensure that a second experienced person knows what is happening on your matter.
If your matter is one which may go to court then I would refer you to paragraph 16 of our Terms of Business which deals with the situation where recovery of legal costs may be possible from another party in the case.

This percentage range does not include the costs of any conveyancing that is required if there is a property to sell. We feel our charges compare favourably with Private Client departments at high street banks and other probate service providers who regularly charge as much as 8% of the total estate plus high conveyancing charges.
